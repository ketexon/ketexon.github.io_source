--- layout: poem title: Mahler's Moon is quite pretty Tonight date_published: 2021-06-25 --- In the clair de lune passing through The sky's tainted window, A laugh wets the ground, A whimper stills the air. The communal delicacy In its imperious schedule, Pings, pangs, and echos down, Plinking across the ribs. The bareness held close The eyes of the bear. The three symphonic sacrifices Could be made with crumbling honor. The conductor draws And the air shakes; The line cut shorter, Retied, rekindled. But silence! Self-fulfillment Is a copper pebble To your diamond scab Atop the pyramid.
